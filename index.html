THE YI

Work on your expansion and flexibility.

Hou Yi (后羿) is a mythological Chinese archer, given the title "Lord Archer". He is sometimes portrayed as a god of archery.

Prone Scapula Retraction.

sets: 1-3. Reps: 8. Freq: every other day. hold: 2sec.

Lie on your stomach, face down, with your arms overhead, elbows bent and thumbs pointing upward.

Lift your elbows and hands off the surface while pulling your shoulder blades together and down your back.

Let your elbows down, and repeat.

Shoulder Flex Supination.

sets: 1-3. Reps: 8. Freq: every other day.

Step on one end of the band and hold the other in your hand.

Tuck your chin in, pull the tip of your shoulder backwards and turn the palm of your hand facing forward, before you raise your arm in front of you, keeping your elbow straight and your head still.

Lower your arm slowly and repeat.

External Rotation.

sets: 1-3. Reps: 8. Freq: every other day. hold: 3 secs.

Stand, and hold the ends of a band in your hands, tuck your elbows in by your sides and bend your elbows 90°.

Have no slack in the band.

Rotate the shoulders by moving your hands away from each other, keeping your elbows tucked in by your sides.

Return and repeat. Pull symmetrically. You can use a mirror to monitor the movement.

SPT 3 - Flexibility.

sets: 3-5. Reps: 6. Freq: every other day. expand: 5-0 secs. rest: 30 secs.

Draw the bow or band in the normal manner and attain the normal holding position, followed by a 10 second continuous expansion at normal expansion speed.

The objective is to draw 1-1.5cm past full draw, without any change in posture.

Do 5 sets of 6 repetitions and take a rest of 30 seconds between each rep, and 2 minutes between each set.

Pectoralis Stretch.

sets: 1. Reps: 3. Freq: daily. rest: 10-20 secs.

Stand straight next to a post or door frame.

Place your hand against the post at shoulder height, as you rotate your body to the opposite side.

Hold the position when you feel a comfortable stretch in front of the shoulder and/or chest.

THE EINARR

Improve your stability and control.

Einarr Thambarskelfir was one of the sharpest of bow-shooters, in the viking-age sagas.

Shoulder Abduction Against a Wall.

sets: 1-3. Reps: 4. Freq: every other day.

Stand with your back and buttocks against the wall. Your feet can be slightly in front.

Place your head (chin tucked), your shoulders, elbows, and wrists against the wall with the shoulders and elbows at 90°.

Keeping the body in contact with the wall, slide your arms up along the wall.

Breathe normally during the movement and slowly return to the initial position.

Face Pull with Band.

sets: 1-3. Reps: 8. Freq: every other day. weight: light band.

Hold the ends of a band anchored to an object located in front of you. With the palms facing down, pull the band toward your neck with the elbows high and the shoulders low.

Keep your back straight and don't poke the chin forward as you pull.

External Rotation with Weight.

sets: 3. Reps: 8. Freq: every other day.

Place your elbow in a 90° elevation on a table beside you, and hold a weight in your hand. Pull the tip of your shoulder backward and lift your hand from the table without lifting the elbow.

Lower your hand slowly and repeat.

Wobble-board Balance.

hold: 2 mins. Freq: every day.

Stand near a wall for support with one foot on the wobble-board or a pillow.

Bring both legs to a neutral stance on the board, and remove your hand from the wall once you have found your balance. Keep balancing for 2 minutes.

You can increase the difficulty by closing your eyes.

THE NEITH

Develop your endurance.

Neith was an ancient Egyptian deity, portrayed as a fierce woman. She is a goddess of war and of hunting, and her emblem is two crossed arrows.

Arm abduction.

sets: 1-3. Reps: 8. Freq: every other day.

Hold one end of a band, palm facing forward and abduct the arm without elevating the shoulder or flexing the elbow. Go to about 90° of abduction, then go back to the starting position and repeat.

Step on the other end of the band with the opposite foot and make sure that you already have tension right at the start of the movement.

SPT 1 - Endurance.

sets: 3. Reps: 6-12. Freq: every other day. hold: 30sec. rest: 1 min.

The bow is drawn in the normal manner, using correct technique, but without an arrow. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute. The rest time between each repetition should be double the hold time.

This exercise must be done with proper form, incorporating aiming, otherwise the bow will move all over the place when fatigue starts to set in.

Neutral Grip Shoulder Press.

sets: 1-3. Reps: 8. Freq: every other day. weight: 2kg

Stand and hold weights in front of your shoulders with your palms facing in.

Lift the weights up and overhead, straightening your elbows to push straight up from the shoulders, then lower under control back to the starting position.

Keep your torso stabilized by engaging the glutes and abdominals to prevent arching your lower back.

THE ARTEMIS

Build power with weight training.

Artemmis is the goddess of the hunt in ancient Greek mythology. Her symbols include a golden bow, arrows and a quiver.

Modified Lateral Raise.

sets: 1-3. Reps: 8 on each arm. Freq: every other day. weight: 1.5kg

Stand with a weight at your side, thumbs up. Raise one arm out to the side. Be sure to stay below impingement level.

The palm should be facing forward with the thumb up.

Hold, then slowly lower the weight to your side and repeat for the required sets and reps.

External Rotation with Weight.

sets: 3. Reps: 8. Freq: every other day.

Place your elbow in a 90° elevation on a table beside you, and hold a weight in your hand. Pull the tip of your shoulder backward and lift your hand from the table without lifting the elbow.

Lower your hand slowly and repeat.

Horizontal Abduction with Weight.

sets: 1-3. Reps: 8 on each arm. Freq: every other day. weight: 1kg.

Lie on your stomach while holding a weight in your hand.

Let your arm hang off the edge of the bed, with the palm facing back and the shoulders down and back.

Lift your right arm up to shoulder level while keeping your shoulder blades squeezed and your thumb down.

Slowly return to the starting position and repeat.

External Rotation, Elbow on Knee.

sets: 1-3. Reps: 8 on each arm. Freq: every other day. weight: 500g.

Sit down on a bench in a 45° angle with one foot flat on the bench and the other flat on the floor.

Support the elbow on the inside portion of the knee (the knee should be slightly lower than the shoulder).

Keep the back straight and use the free hand for support on the bench.

Look directly ahead and rotate the arm down without moving the elbow or knee.

Keep the elbow flexed at 90° and the shoulder stable.

THE ROBIN

Hone your aim and accuracy with classic bow training.

Robin Hood is a legendary heroic in English folklore. He was a highly skilled archer with a perchant for swapping clothes.

SPT 1 - Endurance.

sets: 3. Reps: 6-12. Freq: every other day. hold: 30sec. rest: 1 min.

The bow is drawn in the normal manner, using correct technique, but without an arrow. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute. The rest time between each repetition should be double the hold time.

This exercise must be done with proper form, incorporating aiming, otherwise the bow will move all over the place when fatigue starts to set in.

SPT 2 - Power/Strength.

sets: 3. Reps: 6-12. Freq: every other day. hold: 3-5 sec. rest: 3-5 min.

Draw from the set-up to the holding position; holding must include transfer to the back muscles and skeletal support, and loading like in a normal shot. Hold for 3-5 seconds and let down, but only as far as set-up, then draw straight back to the anchor position and, again, include transfer and loading.

Do this 5 to 12 times for 3 to 5 sets, depending on your current strength.

Rest period between sets is 3 to 5 minutes. Ensure that proper alignment and form is maintained.

SPT 3 - Flexibility.

sets: 3-5. Reps: 6. Freq: every other day. expand: 5-0 secs. rest: 30 secs.

Draw the bow or band in the normal manner and attain the normal holding position, followed by a 10 second continuous expansion at normal expansion speed.

The objective is to draw 1-1.5cm past full draw, without any change in posture.

Do 5 sets of 6 repetitions and take a rest of 30 seconds between each rep, and 2 minutes between each set.
